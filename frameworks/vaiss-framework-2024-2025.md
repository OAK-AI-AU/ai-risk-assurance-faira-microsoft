# Voluntary AI Safety Standard (VAISS)

**Australia's Industry and Government AI Safety Framework**

## Overview

The Voluntary AI Safety Standard (VAISS) is Australia's practical guidance framework helping organisations develop and deploy AI systems safely and reliably. Released by the Australian Government's National AI Centre, VAISS provides implementable standards for responsible AI use across both public and private sectors.

## The 10 Guardrails

VAISS establishes 10 critical guardrails for AI systems that form the foundation of safe and responsible AI:

### 1. Accountability and Governance
Clear ownership structures, decision-making frameworks, and lines of responsibility for AI systems throughout their lifecycle.

**Key Requirements**:
- Designated accountable individuals and teams
- Clear governance structures
- Defined roles and responsibilities
- Executive oversight mechanisms

### 2. Risk Management
Systematic identification, assessment, and mitigation of AI-related risks proportionate to potential impacts.

**Key Requirements**:
- Comprehensive risk assessment processes
- Risk-based approach to AI deployment
- Continuous risk monitoring
- Mitigation strategies for identified risks

### 3. Data Governance and Protection
Responsible data handling, privacy protection, and data quality assurance throughout the AI lifecycle.

**Key Requirements**:
- Data quality standards
- Privacy protection measures
- Data minimization principles
- Secure data handling and storage

### 4. Testing and Monitoring
Continuous evaluation of AI system performance, safety, and reliability.

**Key Requirements**:
- Pre-deployment testing regimes
- Ongoing performance monitoring
- Safety and reliability metrics
- Regular system audits

### 5. Human Oversight
Meaningful human control and intervention capabilities in AI decision-making processes.

**Key Requirements**:
- Human-in-the-loop mechanisms
- Override capabilities
- Clear escalation pathways
- Human review processes for high-risk decisions

### 6. Transparency
Clear communication about AI system operations, capabilities, and limitations to stakeholders.

**Key Requirements**:
- Clear disclosure of AI use
- Explanation of AI capabilities and limitations
- Accessible information for users
- Documentation of AI system design and operation

### 7. Contestability
Mechanisms enabling challenge and appeal of AI-driven decisions.

**Key Requirements**:
- Appeal processes
- Feedback mechanisms
- Review procedures
- Clear channels for contestation

### 8. Supply Chain Transparency
Understanding and managing risks from AI components, dependencies, and third-party providers.

**Key Requirements**:
- Third-party component tracking
- Vendor risk assessment
- Supply chain documentation
- Dependency management

### 9. Compliance
Adherence to relevant laws, regulations, and industry standards.

**Key Requirements**:
- Legal compliance verification
- Regulatory alignment
- Industry standard adoption
- Compliance monitoring and reporting

### 10. Stakeholder Engagement
Inclusive consultation with affected parties and ongoing engagement throughout the AI lifecycle.

**Key Requirements**:
- Stakeholder identification and mapping
- Consultation processes
- Feedback integration
- Ongoing engagement mechanisms

## International Alignment

VAISS is designed to align with international standards and frameworks:

- **ISO/IEC 42001:2023** - AI Management Systems standard
- **NIST AI RMF 1.0** - U.S. National Institute of Standards and Technology AI Risk Management Framework
- **OECD AI Principles** - Organization for Economic Co-operation and Development AI guidelines

This alignment ensures Australian organisations can comply with international laws and standards when following VAISS.

## Application Scope

### AI Deployers
Organizations that use AI systems in their operations:
- Focus on organisational and system-level implementation
- Risk management and governance
- Stakeholder engagement
- Operational safety and monitoring

### AI Developers
Organizations that create AI systems and models:
- Technical safety measures
- Development lifecycle controls
- Testing and validation
- Supply chain management

**Note**: Version 1 primarily focuses on AI deployers at organisational and system level, with Version 2 expected to provide enhanced developer guidance.

## Relationship to Regulatory Framework

## Integration with Australian Frameworks

VAISS integrates seamlessly with other Australian AI governance frameworks:

### National Framework for AI Assurance in Government (NFAAIG)
- NFAAIG mandates AI assurance for government entities
- VAISS provides practical implementation guidance
- Both based on Australia's 8 AI Ethics Principles

### Australian Responsible AI Index 2025
- RAI Index measures maturity across 5 dimensions
- VAISS guardrails map to RAI Index assessment areas
- Organizations can use both for assessment and benchmarking

### Queensland FAIRA Framework
- FAIRA provides detailed risk assessment methodology
- VAISS guardrails align with FAIRA values and controls
- Both frameworks complement each other for comprehensive governance
