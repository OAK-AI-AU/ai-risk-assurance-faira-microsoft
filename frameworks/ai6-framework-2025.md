# AI6 Framework - Australia's 6 Essential AI Practices

**Australia's Primary AI Adoption Guidance for Organisations**

## Overview

The AI6 Framework is Australia's comprehensive guidance for responsible AI adoption, released by the National AI Centre (NAIC) in October 2025. It establishes 6 essential practices that organisations should implement when developing, deploying, or using AI systems.

## Framework Information

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|

| **Publisher** | National AI Centre (NAIC) / Department of Industry, Science and Resources |
| **Version** | 1.0 |
| **Release Date** | October 2025 |
| **Status** | Current |
| **Type** | Voluntary guidance |
| **Scope** | All AI systems deployed in Australia |

## Relationship to Other Frameworks

The AI6 Framework serves as the primary entry point for Australian organisations adopting AI responsibly:

- **Replaces**: The AI6 Framework is designed as the first step for organisations, with VAISS providing more detailed technical implementation guidance
- **Complements**: Works alongside VAISS, FAIRA, and international frameworks
- **Aligns with**: ISO 42001, NIST AI RMF, EU AI Act, OECD AI Principles

## The 6 Essential Practices

### Practice 1: Governance and Accountability

**Establish clear ownership, decision-making frameworks, and accountability for AI systems.**

#### Requirements

- Designate accountable individuals and teams for AI systems
- Establish AI governance structures with executive oversight
- Define clear roles and responsibilities
- Implement AI policy and procedures
- Maintain governance documentation and records

#### VAISS Alignment

| VAISS Guardrail | Alignment |
|-----------------|-----------|
| Guardrail 1: Accountability and Governance | **Full** |
| Guardrail 9: Compliance | Partial |

#### Implementation Guidance

1. Appoint an AI Governance Lead or equivalent role
2. Establish an AI Ethics Committee or governance board
3. Document AI policies covering development, deployment, and use
4. Create RACI matrices for AI system responsibilities
5. Implement regular governance reviews and reporting

---

### Practice 2: Risk Management

**Systematically identify, assess, and mitigate AI-related risks.**

#### Requirements

- Establish AI risk management framework
- Conduct risk assessments for AI systems
- Implement risk mitigation controls
- Monitor and review risks continuously
- Document and report on risk status

#### VAISS Alignment

| VAISS Guardrail | Alignment |
|-----------------|-----------|
| Guardrail 2: Risk Management | **Full** |

#### FAIRA Alignment

- Part A: Components Analysis (risk identification)
- Part C: Controls for AI Risks (mitigation)

#### Implementation Guidance

1. Integrate AI risks into enterprise risk management
2. Use the FAIRA risk assessment methodology
3. Maintain an AI risk register
4. Conduct regular risk reviews (quarterly minimum)
5. Establish risk escalation procedures

---

### Practice 3: Data Quality and Privacy

**Ensure data governance, quality assurance, and privacy protection.**

#### Requirements

- Establish data governance policies
- Ensure training data quality and representativeness
- Implement privacy protection measures
- Apply data minimisation principles
- Secure data handling and storage

#### VAISS Alignment

| VAISS Guardrail | Alignment |
|-----------------|-----------|
| Guardrail 3: Data Governance and Protection | **Full** |

#### Privacy Legislation

- Privacy Act 1988 (Cth)
- Information Privacy Act 2009 (Qld)
- State/territory privacy legislation as applicable

#### Implementation Guidance

1. Conduct data quality assessments before training
2. Document data sources and provenance
3. Implement privacy impact assessments (PIAs)
4. Apply anonymisation/pseudonymisation where appropriate
5. Establish data retention and deletion policies

---

### Practice 4: Testing and Validation

**Continuous evaluation of AI system performance, safety, and reliability.**

#### Requirements

- Establish pre-deployment testing regimes
- Conduct ongoing performance monitoring
- Test for bias and fairness
- Validate safety and reliability
- Conduct regular system audits

#### VAISS Alignment

| VAISS Guardrail | Alignment |
|-----------------|-----------|
| Guardrail 4: Testing and Monitoring | **Full** |

#### Implementation Guidance

1. Develop comprehensive test plans covering:
   - Functional testing
   - Bias and fairness testing
   - Security testing
   - Performance testing
2. Implement monitoring dashboards
3. Establish alert thresholds for model drift
4. Conduct regular audits (internal and external)
5. Document all test results and actions taken

---

### Practice 5: Transparency and Explainability

**Clear communication about AI system operations, capabilities, and limitations.**

#### Requirements

- Disclose AI use to affected parties
- Explain AI capabilities and limitations
- Provide accessible information to users
- Document AI system design and operation
- Enable interpretability where possible

#### VAISS Alignment

| VAISS Guardrail | Alignment |
|-----------------|-----------|
| Guardrail 6: Transparency | **Full** |
| Guardrail 7: Contestability | Partial |
| Guardrail 8: Supply Chain Transparency | Partial |

#### Implementation Guidance

1. Publish AI transparency statements
2. Create user-facing explanations of AI decisions
3. Maintain technical documentation (model cards, datasheets)
4. Implement explainability features where technically feasible
5. Provide clear disclosures when users interact with AI

---

### Practice 6: Human Oversight and Control

**Meaningful human control and intervention capabilities.**

#### Requirements

- Implement human-in-the-loop mechanisms
- Provide override capabilities
- Establish clear escalation pathways
- Enable human review for high-risk decisions
- Support contestability and appeal

#### VAISS Alignment

| VAISS Guardrail | Alignment |
|-----------------|-----------|
| Guardrail 5: Human Oversight | **Full** |
| Guardrail 7: Contestability | **Full** |

#### Implementation Guidance

1. Design AI systems with human intervention points
2. Implement "stop" or "override" capabilities
3. Establish escalation procedures for complex cases
4. Create appeals processes for AI-affected decisions
5. Train staff on when and how to intervene

## Implementation Roadmap

### Phase 1: Assessment (Weeks 1-4)

1. Review current AI systems and use cases
2. Complete the NAIC AI Adoption Screening Tool
3. Conduct gap analysis against 6 practices
4. Prioritise implementation areas

### Phase 2: Planning (Weeks 5-8)

1. Develop implementation plan per practice
2. Assign responsibilities using RACI
3. Allocate resources and budget
4. Establish timeline and milestones

### Phase 3: Implementation (Months 3-6)

1. Roll out governance structures (Practice 1)
2. Implement risk management processes (Practice 2)
3. Establish data governance (Practice 3)
4. Deploy testing frameworks (Practice 4)
5. Create transparency documentation (Practice 5)
6. Implement oversight mechanisms (Practice 6)

### Phase 4: Monitoring (Ongoing)

1. Regular practice compliance reviews
2. Continuous improvement based on feedback
3. Annual comprehensive assessment
4. Update documentation and training
