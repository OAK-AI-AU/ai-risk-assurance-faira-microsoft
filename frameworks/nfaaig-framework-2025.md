# National Framework for the Assurance of Artificial Intelligence in Government (NFAAIG)

**Australian Government AI Governance Framework**

## Overview

The National Framework for the Assurance of Artificial Intelligence in Government (NFAAIG) was agreed to and released by the Data and Digital Ministers Meeting on 21 June 2024. This framework establishes foundations for a nationally consistent approach to AI assurance across federal, state, and territory governments in Australia.

## Purpose

The NFAAIG is a key step towards gaining public confidence and trust in the safe and responsible use of AI by Australia's governments. It provides a uniform set of principles and guidelines for the responsible use of AI across all levels of government.

## Five Cornerstones of AI Assurance

The framework is built on five foundational cornerstones:

### 1. Governance
Establishing clear accountability structures, roles, and responsibilities for AI systems throughout their lifecycle.

### 2. Data Governance
Ensuring responsible data management, privacy protection, and data quality standards for AI systems.

### 3. Risk-Based Approach
Implementing proportionate risk management based on the potential impact and use case of AI systems.

### 4. Standards
Adhering to relevant technical and ethical standards for AI development and deployment.

### 5. Procurement
Incorporating AI assurance requirements into government procurement processes and vendor management.

## Alignment with AI Ethics Principles

The NFAAIG is based on Australia's **8 AI Ethics Principles**, ensuring ethical considerations are embedded throughout the AI lifecycle:

1. Human, Social and Environmental Wellbeing
2. Human-Centred Values
3. Fairness
4. Privacy Protection and Security
5. Reliability and Safety
6. Transparency and Explainability
7. Contestability
8. Accountability

## Integration with Other Frameworks

The NFAAIG complements and aligns with:

- **Queensland FAIRA Framework** - State-level AI risk assessment tool
- **Voluntary AI Safety Standard (VAISS)** - Industry and government AI safety standard
- **Australian Responsible AI Index** - Maturity benchmarking tool
- **ISO/IEC 42001:2023** - International AI management system standard
- **NIST AI Risk Management Framework** - U.S. federal AI risk framework

## Agency Implementation

Government agencies are implementing NFAAIG through various approaches:

### Services Australia Example
Published **Automation and AI Strategy 2025-27** (May 2025) demonstrating alignment with NFAAIG requirements, including:
- Clear governance structures
- Risk management processes
- Stakeholder engagement
- Transparency mechanisms

## Assurance Activities

The framework requires government entities to conduct regular assurance activities:

1. **Risk Assessments** - Comprehensive AI risk evaluation
2. **Impact Assessments** - Analysis of AI system impacts on stakeholders
3. **Transparency Reporting** - Public disclosure of AI use and governance
4. **Continuous Monitoring** - Ongoing performance and safety evaluation
5. **Regular Reviews** - Periodic framework compliance assessment
